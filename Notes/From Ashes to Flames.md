Most of you may know me as a Mechanical Engineer from NUST. 
Some of you may know that I'm a Software Engineer. 

In fact, None of these statements are false. Here's how it has come to this.

After graduation from NUST in BE Mechanical in 2020, I immediately started working as an Tech. Asst. Manager (Mechanical) in a Public Sector Organization. Had a good starting salary and some good perks too. But as the time passed, I started realizing that I wouldn't be able to breathe if It went on like this for too long. I started, exploring hobbies and skills. For some time, found interest in learning to code, freelancing and eventually a part time job. Interest that I lacked in one job, I found in the other. 

*4 years had passed and I become fairly web development tech. and then...*

It was the last week of April 2024, and I headed to my village to enjoy the vacations. For I knew that It might be long time before I could get 2 weeks of leaves. TBH, I didn't really get them approved. I just decided to take 2 weeks off. In normal circumstances, this would've led to a disaster. But I knew all of what is to come and I was ready. I had made up my mind to leave this job.

Now, not saying I'm the first one to experience this. But it was a first one for me. I had spent 4 years as an AM-Mechanical, and still couldn't find my spark in it. Many called it foolishness, but spending a lifetime of doing what you don't find value in, didn't appear to me as wisdom either.

After 2 weeks off from work, I came back to office and met my PD at the entrance of my office. Our conversation wasn't long; at the end of it he asked me to submit my resignation. And that's what I did. I went to my table, opened the drawer, signed the resignation letter lying there for months and submitted it. This was a goodbye to mechanical engineering and the pivot to software engineering.

Starting in April 2024, the process took way too long. I had a remote part-time job at the time of resignation which eventually turned to a full time job in June 2025, 2 months after resignation. I had changed the field in search of passion and growth, and after some time as a remote web developer, I felt that I needed to get a glimpse of how the market works on-ground.

I started applying again in February 2025, and in April 2025, I started working in another startup as a software engineer, primarily in the backend engineering domain. But I hadn't let go of the remote job. I wanted to learn as much as possible as fast as I could. And funnily enough, first few days after I joined the new startup, I finally got a call to receive the relieving letter from the Govt. Job.

*And yes, both the startups knew about the each other and the fulltime commitment. How did I do it? That's a story for another time; and an interesting one too*

As of now, I still actively develop for both startups. And looking back at the leap of faith I took, how far have I come. I didn't know I could actually get this far. I also don't know what would have become of me If I had stayed in my first job as a Mechanical engineer.
One thing I do know is that, I enjoy this life. The scenario might not be something most of you would consider good. But for me its better than yesterday. And I continue to try and make sure that my tomorrow is even better than today. And so the grind continues.
